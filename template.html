BEETLES

Information about them/External Morphology

Head & Mouthpart

The beetle either has a foward or downturned type of mouth, it also has mandibles and palps for chewing or biting. Its head isn't segmented but more like a hard capsule shape with crucial organs and ts compound eyes. some beetles have eyes that can divide.

Thorax

The thorax on the beetle is segmented int two parts, the pro and pterothorax, A pterothorax is the fused metoathorax which is commonly seperated in different insect species.

Legs

Its legs end in two to five sal segments called tarsi. Beetles have claws, usually one pair of claws. Most beetles use their legs for walking like aquatic beetles. Male beetles mostly dive in since they have suctorial cups on its forelegs that use to grasp females.

Wings

Its forewings of beetles are not used for flight, but form elytra which covers the hind part.

Abdomen

Th abdomen is the secion behind the metathorax. Each of them has a hole for breathing and respiration called spiracle and has three different segmented sclerites, the tergum, pluera and the sternum.
